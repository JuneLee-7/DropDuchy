Game Basics

Core Gameplay Loop

The core of Drop Duchy revolves around a dynamic loop:

  • Block Placement: Strategically place falling blocks of various shapes and functions onto the game board to construct your territory and create resource networks.
  • Resource Collection: Completing lines or specific patterns with blocks doesn't clear them in the traditional sense; instead, it often multiplies resource generation from adjacent, compatible structures. Gather essential resources like Wood, Stone, Food, and Gold.
  • Building & Upgrading: Use collected resources to construct a variety of buildings, recruit military units, research new technologies, and upgrade existing structures or cards.
  • Deckbuilding: Acquire and upgrade a diverse set of cards that provide powerful passive bonuses, active abilities, new units, or unique building options. Strategically build your deck to suit your playstyle.
  • Combat & Defense: Raise an army to defend your duchy from encroaching enemies and powerful bosses. Engage in tactical combat where unit placement and counters are key.
  • Roguelike Progression: Each run presents new challenges and opportunities. Even in defeat, you gain experience towards permanent meta-upgrades, unlocking new content and bonuses for future attempts.

Resources

Effectively managing resources is crucial for your duchy's survival and growth. Key resources include (but may not be limited to):

  • Wood: Primarily used for basic buildings and some unit recruitment. Gathered from forest tiles and lumber mills.
  • Stone: Essential for sturdier constructions, defensive structures, and advanced buildings. Quarried from mountainous terrain.
  • Food: Required to sustain your population and recruit/maintain certain units. Produced by farms and compatible terrain.
  • Gold: Used for advanced upgrades, trading at the Market Guild, and maintaining powerful units or structures. Can be generated through markets, trade, or special events.
  • Mana/Energy (Placeholder): A potential special resource used for casting powerful card abilities or fueling magical structures. (Details to be expanded)

Building Your Duchy & Economy

Building Blocks & Terrain Synergy

The foundation of your duchy is built upon the strategic placement of blocks onto diverse terrain types. Each terrain (e.g., Forests, Grasslands, Rivers, Mountains) offers unique benefits or challenges. Placing specific building blocks adjacent to compatible terrain types is key to maximizing resource output and unlocking powerful synergies. For example, a farm benefits greatly from fertile grassland and access to water.

Key Building Types

Your duchy can host a variety of structures, each serving a vital purpose:

  • Resource Production:
    • Farms: Generate Food.
    • Lumber Mills: Produce Wood.
    • Quarries: Mine Stone.
    • Markets: Generate Gold.
  • Military Structures:
    • Barracks: Recruit Swordsmen and Axemen.
    • Archery Ranges: Train Archers.
    • Defensive Walls & Towers: Enhance your duchy's defenses.
  • Technology & Support:
    • Workshops/Smithies: Unlock unit upgrades or new building technologies.
    • Libraries/Academies: Research new cards or global bonuses.
    • Market Guild: Allows trading of resources and acquisition/upgrading of cards.

Many more specialized buildings can be unlocked through progression and card discovery!

Economic Strategy

A thriving economy is the backbone of a strong duchy. Consider these strategic points:

  • Balanced Growth: Ensure a steady income of all necessary resources. Don't over-specialize too early.
  • Efficient Placement: Maximize terrain synergies to boost resource production without overextending.
  • Expansion vs. Consolidation: Know when to expand your territory to claim new resources and when to focus on fortifying your current holdings.
  • Market Opportunities: Utilize the Market Guild effectively to offload surplus resources or acquire those you're lacking.

Combat System

Core Mechanics

  • Unit Counters (Rock-Paper-Scissors): Combat heavily relies on a counter system:
    • Archers deal bonus damage to Axemen.
    • Axemen deal bonus damage to Swordsmen.
    • Swordsmen deal bonus damage to Archers.
    Exploiting these matchups is critical for success. A countered unit will effectively perform worse, while a countering unit gains a significant advantage.
  • Unit Merging: When two friendly military unit groups are placed adjacent to each other, they merge. The resulting group will adopt the type of the unit that was most numerous. For example, 10 Archers merging with 5 Swordsmen will result in a group of 15 Archers. Enemies also merge, so anticipate and sometimes manipulate these merges to your advantage.

Unit Types

  • Swordsmen: Standard melee infantry, effective against Archers. Form the backbone of many early-game armies.
  • Axemen: Heavy melee infantry, specialized in breaking through lines and countering Swordsmen.
  • Archers: Ranged units that can attack from a distance, crucial for dealing with Axemen and providing fire support.
  • (More specialized units can be unlocked via cards and technology!)

Enemy Types & Behaviors

You will face a variety of foes, from basic grunts spawned by certain blocks to elite units and powerful bosses. Enemies may have different attack patterns, speeds, and resistances. Understanding their behavior is key to effective defense. (Details on specific enemies will be added as they are "revealed").

Bosses

Periodically, powerful Bosses will challenge your duchy. These encounters are unique and often feature special mechanics that require adaptive strategies. Defeating them yields significant rewards. Examples include the formidable guardians of the "Shifting Sands" biome.

Combat Tips & Strategies

  • Know Your Counters: Always try to engage enemy units with your own units that counter them.
  • Strategic Positioning: Use terrain to your advantage. Chokepoints can limit enemy numbers, while high ground might benefit ranged units.
  • Reserve Enemy Spawners: If possible, hold an enemy-spawning block in your reserve to reduce the number of immediate threats.
  • Focus Fire: Concentrate your army's attacks on high-threat targets or to quickly eliminate weakened enemy groups.
  • Anticipate Merges: Be mindful of how enemy units might merge and try to prevent them from forming overwhelmingly large groups of a single dangerous type.
  • Utilize Cards: Combat-focused cards can turn the tide by buffing your units, debuffing enemies, or providing direct damage.

Cards & Deckbuilding

Overview

Cards are a central pillar of Drop Duchy's strategy, offering immense variety and replayability. With over 80 unique cards (and more added in updates like the "Ancient Relics" pack!), your deck defines your approach to building, economy, and warfare.

Card Types

  • Building Cards: These cards might unlock new building types, enhance existing ones (e.g., "Artisan's Toolkit" improving resource block synergies), or provide global bonuses to your construction efforts.
  • Technology Cards: Grant access to new technologies, passive upgrades for your duchy, or unlock advanced unit types/abilities.
  • Unit Enhancement Cards: Directly improve the stats or abilities of your military units (e.g., increased damage for Archers, more health for Swordsmen).
  • Resource Cards: Can provide instant resource infusions, improve resource generation rates, or alter how resources are gathered.
  • Spell/Ability Cards: Offer powerful, often single-use effects, such as direct damage to enemies, defensive buffs, or manipulation of the game board.

Acquiring & Upgrading Cards

  • Progression Unlocks: Many cards are unlocked by leveling up your player profile or completing specific in-game challenges.
  • Market Guild: The Market Guild is a key location where you can spend Gold to purchase new cards from a rotating selection or upgrade cards you already own.
  • In-Game Events & Rewards: Certain events or defeating powerful enemies might also reward you with new cards.

Deckbuilding Strategy

Crafting an effective deck is an art form in Drop Duchy:

  • Synergy is Key: Look for cards that complement each other. A deck focused on wood production might include cards that boost lumber mills, reduce wood costs for buildings, and enhance units that benefit from forest terrain.
  • Adapt to Your Faction: Each faction may have unique cards or benefit more from certain card types.
  • Early vs. Late Game: Consider cards that provide early advantages versus those that offer powerful late-game scaling.
  • Counter a Nemesis: If you consistently struggle against certain enemy types or bosses, try to include cards in your deck that specifically help counter them.
  • Don't Over-Specialize (Usually): While a focused deck can be powerful, ensure you have some flexibility to adapt to unexpected challenges.

  • The Republic (Merchants/Innovators):

  • The Cult (Zealots/Mystics):
    • Characteristics: Likely to delve into more esoteric or unconventional strategies, perhaps utilizing unique magical abilities, sacrificial mechanics, or specialized, powerful-but-costly units. Their playstyle might be high-risk, high-reward.
    • Potential Unique Quests/Rewards: Could involve forbidden rituals, spreading their influence, or harnessing dark powers. Rewards might include unique spell cards, faction-specific unit transformations, or powerful artifacts.

Biomes & Environments

The world of Drop Duchy is composed of various biomes, each presenting unique tactical considerations, resource availabilities, and potential threats.

  • Standard Biomes:
    • Forests: Rich in Wood. May offer cover or hinder movement for certain units. Ideal for Lumber Mills.
    • Grasslands: Fertile land suitable for Farms and general construction. Often open terrain.
    • Mountains/Hills: Source of Stone. Can provide defensive advantages or act as chokepoints. Suitable for Quarries.
    • Lakes/Rivers: Can provide Food bonuses (fishing) or be integrated into defensive strategies. May require specific buildings (bridges) to cross.
    • Villages: May offer unique interaction points, quests, or recruitment opportunities.
  • Special Biomes:
    • The Shifting Sands Biome: A challenging desert environment introduced in Update v1.2.0. Features unique hazards (e.g., quicksand, sandstorms), new enemy types adapted to the desert, and formidable bosses. Resource availability might be scarce or different from standard biomes.
    • (Future updates may introduce more unique biomes like Volcanic Wastes, Frozen Tundras, or Corrupted Lands, each with their own rules and content.)

Meta-Progression & Challenges

Player Level & Permanent Unlocks

Even when a run ends in defeat, your efforts contribute to your overall meta-progression. By gaining experience points (XP) from playing, you will level up your player profile. Each level up can grant permanent bonuses and unlocks, such as:

  • New Cards: Adding new strategic options to your potential deck pool for all future runs.
  • Starting Resource Boosts: Begin future runs with slightly more Wood, Gold, or other key resources.
  • Faction-Specific Perks: Enhancements that apply when playing a particular faction.
  • Unlockable Game Features: Access to new game modes, higher difficulty levels, or new starting options.
  • Increased Vitality/Base Stats: Minor permanent improvements to your core duchy's resilience.

This system ensures that every game played is a step forward, making you progressively stronger and better equipped for the challenges ahead.

The Challenge System

Drop Duchy features a robust system of 100 distinct challenges. These challenges encourage players to explore different strategies, achieve specific milestones, or overcome difficult scenarios. Examples might include:

  • Winning a run with a specific faction.
  • Defeating a boss under certain conditions.
  • Building a duchy that reaches a certain economic output.
  • Utilizing a particular card synergy to great effect.
  • Surviving for a set amount of time against overwhelming odds.

Completing challenges rewards players with substantial XP boosts, unique cosmetic unlocks (if applicable), or even rare and powerful cards, further fueling meta-progression and offering tangible goals beyond simply winning a run.
